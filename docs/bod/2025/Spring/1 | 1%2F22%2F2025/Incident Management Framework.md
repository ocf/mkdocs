Questions to answer:

* What to do in cases of harassment?
* What to do if there's a dispute?


Staff should not feel compelled to intervene in an ongoing dispute. General managers are answerable to handling incidents. Elevating concerns to GMs is an appropriate method to respond to someone bringing up an issue, and is an appropriate avenue to discuss concerns with users or members.


## Framework

* Classifications:
  * Dispute
  * Harassment
  * Suspect
* To document:
  * Name (or description)
  * What happened
  * Date and time
  * Who was involved
  * Who were witness


Most things should be handled without resorting to this! This is the micro-nuclear option! Part of the general manager's role description is to manage lab culture and interpersonal incidents.


Incidents should only be reviewed by managers, for privacy.


## Motivation

This framework was motivated by past incidents of harassment, and recent disputes. 

* Several members have been banned from the OCF, and a few have been physically asked to leave. When coming to decisions about banning these members, documentation helps for:
  * Coming to decisions during BoD hearings (and explaining our reasons to the person in question)
  * Justifying our decisions to regulatory parties (ASUC, Berkeley) in case of dispute
  * Furnishing analytics for what kinds of incidents Opstaff and Volstaff should be prepared for
